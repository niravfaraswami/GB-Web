QUESTIONS?

COMMON QUESTIONS

How long does it take to ferment? +
3–5 days at typical Indian room temperature (25–32°C). Cooler weather may extend it to 6–7 days. The bubbles in the airlock tell you when it's active. Taste from day 3 onwards.
Is this really probiotic? +
Yes. Wild lacto-fermentation produces 8+ strains of beneficial Lactobacillus bacteria. By day 5, a well-fermented kanji has ~10⁹ CFU/ml live cultures.
What vegetables can I use? +
Black carrots and beetroot are the traditional kanji base. The recipe book includes 6 other variations: cucumber-mint, amla-turmeric, aam panna, watermelon rind, pineapple, and a mixed seasonal version.
What's in the Ferment Boost? +
Inulin + FOS + Resistant Dextrin — three prebiotic fibers that feed the good bacteria. This is what cuts ferment time from 7–10 days (traditional) to 3–5 days.
How long does it last? +
Once ready, swap to the storage lid and refrigerate. Refrigerated kanji stays good for 2 weeks. The 2L jar typically lasts a household 10–14 days.
Can I reuse the jar? +
The jar lasts years — borosilicate glass, dishwasher-safe. The airlock is reusable indefinitely. Only spice mix and ferment boost are consumables. Refill spice mix sold separately at ₹449 for 5 batches.
How much should I drink daily? +
Start with 50ml/day for the first week. Most users settle at 100–150ml daily. More than 200ml/day is unnecessary — your gut microbiome takes time to populate.
Is it safe for kids? +
Yes — kanji is a traditional Indian drink, generally safe for kids 5+ in moderate amounts (50–100ml/day). Probiotics support developing guts. Consult your pediatrician for personalized guidance.
