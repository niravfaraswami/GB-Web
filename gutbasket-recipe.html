Raw Mango Probiotic Achaar

Method

Read all steps before starting. Set up everything you need in advance — fermentation goes wrong when you scramble for ingredients mid-recipe.

1 🥭
Wash and chop the mangoes
Wash 900g of unripe green mangoes thoroughly. Don't peel — the skin holds the wild lactic acid bacteria you need. Chop into 2cm cubes. The pieces should be small enough to fit through a teaspoon comfortably.
2 🧂
Salt and let them sweat
In a large bowl, mix the chopped mangoes with 20g salt. Massage gently for 2 minutes. The salt pulls water out of the mango — this becomes your fermentation brine. Let sit 30 minutes.
3 🌶️
Add the spice mix
Add the mustard seeds, red chilli, fennel, turmeric, and fenugreek. Mix thoroughly. The mango should be coated evenly. Add 1 tsp of Ferment Boost — this gives the lactic acid bacteria food on day 1.
4 🫙
Pack into the jar
Transfer the mixture into a clean fermenter jar. Press down hard with a clean spoon to release more brine. Top up with filtered water if needed — the mango must be fully submerged. Place a glass weight on top to keep everything below the brine line.
5 ⏳
Seal and wait
Attach the airlock lid. Place the jar in a cool spot (20–25°C). Within 24 hours you'll see bubbles forming. Don't open the jar — the airlock handles all gas release.
6 🍽️
Taste from day 5
On day 5, open the jar and taste a small piece. If it's tangy and crunchy, transfer to the fridge. If you want more tang, leave another 1–2 days. Once refrigerated, the achaar keeps for 2–3 months.

Notes & variations

Tips for adapting the recipe, troubleshooting common issues, and substitutions.

  • For a milder version: reduce red chilli to 3g and skip fenugreek.
  • For a sweeter take: add 1 tsp jaggery powder along with the salt — note this slows fermentation by ~1 day.
  • If you see white film on the surface (kahm yeast), skim it off and continue. It's harmless.
  • Don't use iodised salt or chlorinated tap water — both kill the bacteria you want.
