HOW TO MAKE

4 STEPS.
FRESH SPROUTS.

▶ WATCH · 60 SEC
1
SOAK THE SEEDS
Add 2 tbsp seeds to the jar. Cover with water. Soak 6–8 hours overnight.
2
DRAIN + RINSE
Tilt jar to drain through the sprouting lid. Rinse twice. Lay jar at angle on the stand.
3
RINSE TWICE DAILY
Morning + evening. Drain fully each time. Keep at room temp, no direct sunlight.
4
HARVEST IN 2–3 DAYS
Sprouts are ready when tails are 1–2 cm long. Refrigerate. Eat over a week.

QUESTIONS?

COMMON
QUESTIONS.

How is this different from sprouting in cloth?+
Cloth holds moisture against the seeds, which causes mould and that musty smell. The mesh lid drains water completely after every rinse — seeds get airflow, no standing water. Cleaner, faster, no smell.
Do I need sunlight?+
No. Sprouts grow best at room temperature, away from direct sunlight. A kitchen counter works perfectly.
Which seeds can I sprout in this jar?+
Moong, chickpea, alfalfa, fenugreek (methi), mustard, broccoli, radish, lentils. Avoid kidney beans (toxic when raw-sprouted) and any seed not labeled sprouting-grade.
How often do I rinse?+
Twice a day — morning and evening. Drain fully each time. The whole process takes under 30 seconds per rinse.
How long do harvested sprouts last?+
Refrigerated, 5–7 days. Rinse before storing and pat dry. Eat raw in salads, stir-fry briefly, or add to khichdi at the end.
Is the jar dishwasher-safe?+
Yes — borosilicate glass and stainless steel mesh both go in the dishwasher. Hand-wash also works.
Can kids do this?+
Yes — it's a great kitchen science project. The transformation from seed to sprout in 2–3 days is genuinely fun to watch.
What if my seeds don't sprout?+
Most often the seeds were too old or stored in heat. We replace any non-germinating seed pack within 30 days — just write to support@gutbasket.com with a photo.
