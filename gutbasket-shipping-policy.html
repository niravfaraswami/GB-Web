Live cultures, dry shipping

No cold chain. By design.

Many fermented-food brands ship cold because they sell finished ferments. We ship dry components — spice mixes, salt, prebiotic boost, recipe book, jar, airlock. The cultures are wild on the vegetables you'll buy at home, plus the LAB starter in the Ferment Boost.

Our Ferment Boost has a 12-month room-temperature shelf life at viability ≥10⁹ CFU/g. The LAB are dormant in dry form; they wake up when you add water and salt. This is why our packaging is light, our shipping is fast, and our carbon cost is low.

DVS dairy cultures are the one exception — those ship cold-chain via insulated mailers and travel only within metro zones. Mentioned on each dairy SKU.

Why this matters. A cold-chain shipment from Gandhinagar to Bangalore costs ~₹450 and arrives in a single-use insulated box. Our standard kit ships in a recycled-pulp carton for ~₹70 and gets you the same end product, alive in your fridge.

Edge cases

Lost, delayed, returned-to-origin.

My parcel hasn't moved in 4 days.
Message us with the tracking number. We'll open a DTDC investigation (their SLA is 48 hours). 90% of stuck parcels are found within 2 days at a hub mis-sort. If the parcel is still untraceable on Day 14 from dispatch, we ship a free replacement.
The address is wrong / I want it sent somewhere else.
If the order hasn't shipped (i.e. no tracking link yet) — yes, we can change it. WhatsApp within 4 hours of placing the order. After dispatch, the parcel will return to us if undeliverable; we then re-ship to the new address at no extra cost.
The delivery agent didn't try to deliver and marked it "address not found".
Message us. We re-initiate delivery at no extra cost. If the agent repeatedly fails, we route the next attempt through India Post Speed, which has better last-mile coverage in some regions.
I'm in the North-East — why does it take so long?
DTDC's network thins out east of Guwahati. We use India Post Speed for Arunachal, Mizoram, Nagaland, Manipur, and Tripura because their coverage is better there, even though their tracking is sparser. We're working on a regional partner — should improve in late 2026.
Do you ship internationally?
Not yet. Our APEDA registration (for export of agri-products) is filed and expected to be approved in Q3 2026. First international corridors planned: UAE, Singapore, UK. Sign up for the newsletter to hear when it launches.
