Our Story · 03

We source like food scientists, ship like a home cook.

Every spice, salt, seed, and culture in a GutBasket kit can be traced to a named supplier within 48 hours. Three sourcing rules, fifteen named partners, and a three-stage quality protocol. Here's the whole map.

15 · NAMED SUPPLIERS100% · ANNUAL AUDITS<48 hrs · BATCH TRACEABILITY
Three rules

How we decide where things come from.

Every supplier has to clear all three. We've turned down twelve so far in 2026 alone.

RULE 01

Traceable to a single batch

If a kit ever fails QC, we need to know within 48 hours which farm, mill, or culture vial it came from. Suppliers who don't lot-code their output don't make the list.

RULE 02

No irradiation, no fumigation

Irradiated spices are technically safe and microbiologically clean. They also kill the volatile compounds that make a ferment taste like food. We pay 2–3× more for non-irradiated.

RULE 03

Indian-first, then world-best

If a competitive supplier exists in India, we use them. If the global benchmark is decisively elsewhere — Estonian fresh yeast, Korean gochugaru — we import. We don't import for image.

Provenance map

The fifteen partners behind the kits.

Each name below is a real supplier with a written contract, an annual audit, and a paper trail. We name them publicly because we have nothing to hide.

KHEDA · GUJARAT

Black mustard seeds (rai)

Co-op of 18 farmers in the Mahi belt. 6 km from our facility. The mustard in every Kanji and Achaar kit. Cold-pressed within 72 hours of harvest, never irradiated.

PUNJAB · BORDER REGION

Black carrots (kala gajar)

Cooperative of growers across Patiala and Sangrur. November–February only. The pigment that makes Kanji crimson is anthocyanin, which doubles as a polyphenol prebiotic.

TUTICORIN · TAMIL NADU

Sea salt (non-iodised)

Solar evaporation pans on the Coromandel coast. We test every truckload for moisture content (must be <0.5%), heavy metals, and iodine (must be near-zero — iodine inhibits LAB).

SALUTAGUSE · ESTONIA

Fresh baker's yeast

One of the world's last factories making fresh-cake yeast at a quality level we couldn't replicate domestically. Air-shipped weekly. Used in our Track C baking line.

JEONJU · SOUTH KOREA

Gochugaru (Korean chilli flakes)

Coarse-ground from a single mill in Jeonju. Sun-dried, never machine-dried. The colour comes from capsanthin, which the LAB don't touch — flavour stays vivid through fermentation.

BIDAR · KARNATAKA

Inulin from chicory root

The prebiotic fibre in our Ferment Boost. Sourced from a chicory grower's collective in north Karnataka. Lab-verified inulin content of 91–94% per batch.

DENMARK · DAIRY CULTURES

DVS yogurt & kefir cultures

Direct-Vat-Set culture vials from a leading Danish bioscience company. Strain-defined, viability-tested, refrigerated through the cold chain to our packing room.

CHENNAI

Glass jars & airlocks

Borosilicate glass from a Chennai-based food-grade manufacturer. Each jar's batch is etched on the base. Airlocks injection-moulded in food-grade silicone next door.

Quality protocol

Every batch is tested three times.

Once at intake, once mid-process, once before it ships. If any check fails, the batch is held — sometimes destroyed.

Stage 1 · Raw material intake

Moisture, heavy metals (Pb, Cd, Hg, As), pesticide residue panel, microbial counts. Spices also get a volatile-oil assay to confirm they aren't irradiated. Failed lots are returned to supplier.

Stage 2 · Mid-process

During spice-blend mixing and culture preparation: weight check, particle-size distribution, moisture re-check. Culture vials are plate-counted to confirm viability before they go into the kit.

Stage 3 · Pre-ship

Every 25th kit pulled for: seal integrity, sachet weight tolerance, pouch barrier test, and a 5-day mock-ferment using kit contents. Only after this passes does the lot release for dispatch.

Stage 4 · Retain & recall

One sealed kit per batch is retained for 18 months. If a customer ever raises an issue, we can compare their kit to its retained sibling. Full batch-trace ready in under 48 hours.

Lab partners

Independent. NABL-accredited.

We don't run our own microbial assays in-house. The conflict of interest would be obvious. Instead, every quarter we send a randomised sample set to two NABL-accredited labs and publish the results.

Our active partners: Eurofins India (Bangalore) for microbiology and viability counts, SGS India (Vadodara) for heavy metals and pesticide panels, and Reliable Analytical Laboratories (Mumbai) for moisture and proximate analysis.

Public test reports. The last four quarters of microbiology and heavy-metals reports are downloadable from the certifications page. We post them whether they look good or not.

Annual audits

We visit every supplier, every year.

Not a desk audit. An actual van, an actual notebook, an actual factory floor. The trip lead is our QA head, Dr. Meena Rajagopalan.

The audit covers: hygiene practices and pest control, traceability paperwork, worker conditions, payment records to upstream farmers, and a side-by-side blind taste of last batch vs current batch.

If a supplier fails the audit, we put them on a 90-day improvement plan. If they fail twice, we move on. We've moved on from four suppliers since launch — three for traceability gaps, one for a fumigation issue.

"You can't certify your way to a good ferment. You have to know the people who grew the seeds."
